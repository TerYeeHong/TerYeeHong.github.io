About

Development time : 3 weeks

Team : 1 people

Software : Unity

Target Platform: PC


This was an assignment by the school and also my first ever experience with shaders. Using Unity, we were to make shaders in HLSL, no shader graphs are used for this project. The assignment required us to make multiple textures, lightings, shadows from scratch. Things that I self learn out of scope includes, terrain scanning effect, voronoi noise for water, and I attempted to recreate the cell shading character shaders that Genshin Impact has.

How to use the Program

W - switch Camera

Q - switch and compare Model

A - activate Terrain scanning

Cell Shading

I will be the first to admit, I could not recreate fully the shader that genshin used even after days and nights of binging tutorials and wrapping my head around the concept of cell shading. In the end, I have perhaps only 80% of the visual aesthetics. I have an outline shader, toon shader, directional shader casting on the face. But i could not replicate the curved shadow that characters can sometimes have on their face at certain angles. It needed to use a texture to map the curve shadow which I had but could not put it into use. Though, I am still quite proud of what I managed to achieve in the short time I had.

Lighting and Shadows

Lighting and shadows was made from scratch by creating an imaginary light point in the shader script, then calculating the resultant albedo diffuse to achieve lighting and shadow effects. I managed to make multiple light sources and multiple shadows with reference to opengl lighting tutorials on youtube.

Extra features

Some extra features that I was inspired to do includes the following.

Water: using voronoi noise

Terrain Scanning: seen in games such as Genshin Impact and Apex Legends, Bloodhound's ability.


Overall, This has been a great learning project. Initially I despised shaders as it seemed complicated and that you could not debug as the code is computed in the GPU. As of now, Shader has really piqued my interest and I definitely want to expand my knowledge in the field of game graphics and its possibilites.
